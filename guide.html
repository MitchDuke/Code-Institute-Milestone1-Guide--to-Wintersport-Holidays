Great places to go on a winter holiday

It's worth noting that all of these resorts offer a variety of slopes and activities for skiers and snowboarders of all levels, not just beginners. However, for beginners, it's important to choose a resort that offers a good range of gentle slopes and quality ski schools to help you get started.

Great resorts of Europe

Andorra

Andorra, set in the Pyrenees between France and Spain is a great place for beginners, intermediate and advanced skiers and snowboarders alike. It has a large piste area and is good value for the money.

Pros:
  • Great for beginners with many easy and wide slopes
  • Good value compared to other European resorts
  • Great ski schools and instructors
Cons:
  • Long transfer times
  • Small compared to the more expensive resorts
  • Road access liable to closures

Bulgaria

Bulgaria is a great place to learn how to ski, with excellent ski schools and loads of tree-lined beginner and intermediate slopes. Its brilliant value so your spending money will go a lot further here.

Pros:
  • Great for beginners with many easy and wide slopes
  • Good value compared to other European resorts
  • Lots of non-skiing activities, such as night skiing and snowmobiling
Cons:
  • Can be crowded during peak season
  • Can suffer from lack of snow in warmer winters
  • Limited slopes and skiing areas

France

France is a firm favourite for ski holidays, loved for its excellent food and diverse, easy-to-reach resorts, from traditional to purpose-built, luxury to budget-friendly.

Pros:
  • Great for beginners with many easy and wide slopes
  • Good value compared to other European resorts
  • Lots of non-skiing activities, such as night skiing and snowmobiling
Cons:
  • Can be crowded during peak season
  • Can suffer from lack of snow in warmer winters
  • Limited slopes and skiing areas

Great resorts from the rest of the world

Canada

Canada has some of the best snow conditions in the world, a lengthy winter with guaranteed snowfall. The many resorts are large and cater to all skill levels. Many of the resorts are situated within national parks giving many spectacular views and scenery.

Pros:
  • Great for beginners with many easy and wide slopes
  • Good value compared to other European resorts
  • Great ski schools and instructors
Cons:
  • Long transfer times
  • Small compared to the more expensive resorts
  • Road access liable to closures

Japan

Japan is a great place to learn how to ski, with long and not very steep runs across many of the resorts. Great powder and snowfall nearly everyday and in some places up to 15m can fall over the course of the season. Tie that up with great food and views of Mt Fuji will make for a memorable trip.

Pros:
  • Great for beginners with many easy and wide slopes
  • Good value compared to other European resorts
  • Lots of non-skiing activities, such as night skiing and snowmobiling
Cons:
  • Can be crowded during peak season
  • Can suffer from lack of snow in warmer winters
  • Limited slopes and skiing areas

USA

The USA is home to thousands of acres of quiet slopes and big, fast chairlifts and gondolas. The mountains are packed with piste for all abilities. If budget isn't an issue the resorts of Colorado sit at high altitude, so there’s lots of light powder. Due to the size of the piste areas these are some of the quietest slopes you will find.

Pros:
  • Great for beginners with many easy and wide slopes
  • Good value compared to other European resorts
  • Lots of non-skiing activities, such as night skiing and snowmobiling
Cons:
  • Can be crowded during peak season
  • Can suffer from lack of snow in warmer winters
  • Limited slopes and skiing areas
